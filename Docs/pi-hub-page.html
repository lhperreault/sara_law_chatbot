Texas Law

Your Rights After an Accident in Texas

You have protections under Texas law. Knowing them can make or break your case.

You have the right to choose your own doctor.

The other driver's insurance company cannot tell you which doctor to see. You get to pick the medical providers you trust. And if you can't afford treatment right now, we can connect you with doctors who will treat you now and get paid when your case resolves.

You have the right to refuse a recorded statement.

The other driver's insurance adjuster will call you — sometimes within hours of the accident. They'll ask you to give a recorded statement. You are not required to do this. Anything you say can be used to reduce or deny your claim. Talk to a lawyer before you talk to their insurance company.

You have two years to file.

Texas law gives you two years from the date of the accident to file a personal injury lawsuit. That might sound like a long time, but evidence disappears, witnesses forget details, and medical records need to be gathered. The sooner you call, the stronger your case.

You can still recover even if you were partly at fault.

Texas follows a modified comparative fault rule. As long as you were less than 51% responsible for the accident, you can still recover compensation — it just gets reduced by your percentage of fault. Don't assume you have no case because the accident wasn't 100% the other driver's fault.
